What is a deck footing, and what does it do ​

A deck footing is a structural element of a deck. A concrete pad sits on the ground and supports the deck's weight. The footing must be appropriately sized and installed to support the weight of the deck and its occupants. Improperly installed or sized deck footings can lead to deck collapse and injuries.

Most decks are built with several support posts on top of the footings. The posts bear the weight of the deck and transfer it to the footings. The size and number of support posts will depend on the size and design of the deck.

The soil type and conditions will also play a role in determining the size and design of the footings. Soil that is loose or sandy may require more extensive or more profound foundations than compacted or clay-like soil. The weight of the deck, the live load (weight of people and furniture), and the dead load (weight of the materials) must all be considered when designing the footings.

Deck footings are typically made of concrete but can also be made of brick, stone, or other materials. The footing must extend below the frost line to prevent heaving during freezing weather. It is essential to consult with a professional before designing and installing deck footings.

​How to choose the right footing for your deck

Building a deck can be fun and rewarding, but it's essential to ensure you select the right footing for your deck. There are several factors to consider when choosing the right footing, and it's necessary to get it right to ensure that your deck is safe and stable. Here are some tips on how to choose the right footing for your deck:

  • The first thing to consider is the soil type. If you have heavy clay soil, you'll need to use a different footing than if you have sandy soil. This is because clay soils are more likely to shift and settle, which can cause your deck to become unstable.
  • Another factor to consider is the weight of your deck. If you have a heavy deck, you'll need to use a stronger footing to support it.
  • You also need to consider the climate. If you live in an area with severe weather conditions, you'll need to use a different footing than if you live in a more moderate climate. This is because extreme weather can cause damage to your deck, and you need to ensure that your foundation can withstand the conditions.
  • You need to think about the aesthetics of your deck. You want it to look good, so you'll need to choose a footing that compliments the style of your deck.

Once you've considered all of these factors, you'll be able to choose the right footing for your deck. Get it right, and you'll have a safe and stable deck that will last for years.

Tips for installing footings correctly. ​

Installing footings correctly is critical for the stability of a building or structure. A few essential tips should be followed to ensure proper installation:

First, the excavated hole should be the correct size and shape and be checked for level before any footings are set in place. The footing itself should also be level, and it should be placed in the center of the hole. Once the footing is in place, concrete should be poured around it to secure it. The concrete should be allowed to cure for at least 24 hours before any construction is started on top of the footing.

Another important tip is to ensure the footing is placed at the correct depth. The depth will vary depending on the type of soil and the height of the building or structure. In general, footings should be placed below the frost line to prevent them from heaving during freeze-thaw cycles.

If you are unsure how to install footings, it is best to consult a professional contractor or engineer. They will be able to assess the situation and give you specific instructions on how to proceed. These tips will help ensure that your footings are installed correctly and that your building or structure is safe and stable.

Maintenance tips for keeping your deck in good condition


Most people don't think about their deck's regular maintenance to stay in good condition. Like any other outdoor furniture, your deck needs to be cleaned and treated with a sealant at least once a year to protect it from the elements. Here are some tips for keeping your deck looking great all year long:

  • Sweep your deck regularly to remove dirt, leaves, and other debris.
  • Clean your patio with a mild soap and water solution at least once a year.
  • Stain or seal your deck to protect it from the sun, rain, and other weather elements.
  • Repair any cracks or splinters in your deck immediately.

These simple tips can help keep your deck looking new for years to come.
